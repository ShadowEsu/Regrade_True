# Regrade 2.0 Design Direction

The attached light-mode boards are the approved reference. Regrade 2.0 should feel like a native student command center: bright, calm, precise, rewarding, and trustworthy. It is not a glassmorphism redesign and not a page full of blue gradients.

## Visual principles

- White and pale-gray canvases provide most of the surface area.
- Regrade blue indicates navigation, progress, links, and the primary action.
- Warm yellow identifies attention—not general decoration.
- Mint/green indicates completed or supported evidence; red is reserved for warnings or deductions.
- Lavender supports AI/help and secondary progress.
- Near-black text carries the hierarchy; muted ink is used sparingly.
- Cards are compact, softly bordered, lightly shadowed, and meaningful.
- Editorial serif is reserved for key messages and success moments; product UI uses a modern sans-serif.

## Interaction principles

The Home screen behaves as a stack of useful moments rather than a static report. Attention and recent-work cards are horizontally browseable; streak and history items expand; detail views rise as sheets or transition from their source card. Motion communicates import, analysis, completion, or failure and never masks missing data.

## Product truth

Recovered marks, completed imports, connector success, subscription state, parent access, and notification events must come from real records. No appeal is sent without explicit final approval.
