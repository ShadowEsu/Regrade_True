# Australian privacy requirements

Draft issue list for qualified Australian counsel and institutional privacy review; not legal advice.

## Australian Privacy Principles

The OAIC’s current APP Guidelines cover open/transparent management, anonymity/pseudonymity, collection, notice, use/disclosure, overseas disclosure, security, access and correction. Regrade should map each data field and processor to APP 1–13 and document the lawful/authorized purpose. Source: [OAIC APP Guidelines](https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/australian-privacy-principles/australian-privacy-principles-guidelines).

## Required decisions before pilot

1. Determine whether the Privacy Act 1988/APPs apply to each entity and which state/territory public-sector or health/education privacy laws also apply.
2. Complete a privacy impact assessment; identify collection authority/consent and give age-appropriate notice.
3. Minimize to the learner’s marked work. Do not collect broad SIS, medical, welfare, attendance, finance or family-contact data.
4. Disclose every overseas recipient/processing location and assess APP 8 accountability/contract controls. “Australian data residency” must not be claimed unless every storage, log, backup, AI and support path is verified.
5. Set active and backup retention, exam/account deletion, legal exceptions and pilot close-out evidence.
6. Define access/correction, complaints, support and identity verification.
7. Establish Notifiable Data Breaches assessment/escalation and school notification obligations.
8. Review direct marketing/analytics/push use; do not use education content for ads or unrelated profiling.
9. Contract AI/subprocessors for security, retention, training use, human access, onward transfer, deletion and incidents.

## AI in schools

The Australian Framework for Generative AI in Schools emphasizes teaching/learning, wellbeing, transparency, fairness, accountability and privacy/security/safety. Regrade should present AI suggestions with evidence and uncertainty, allow human challenge, and never position automated output as a final grade decision. Source: [Australian Framework for Generative AI in Schools](https://www.education.gov.au/schooling/announcements/australian-framework-generative-artificial-intelligence-ai-schools).

Professional review is still required for child consent/capacity, educational records, copyright in exam materials, automated decision risks, cross-border transfer, consumer/subscription terms and each institution’s policy.
