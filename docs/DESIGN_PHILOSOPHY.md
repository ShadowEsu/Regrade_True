# Regrade 2.0 Design Philosophy

## Positioning

Regrade is a serious academic workspace with a humane assistant. It should feel credible enough for a university partnership, clear enough for a student under stress, and warm enough that Mr Whale still belongs.

## Principles

### Evidence first

The most important visible object is the student's marked work and the evidence extracted from it. Decoration must never outrank source material, appeal strength, or uncertainty.

### One screen, one decision

Each destination gets one primary action. Secondary actions live in rows, contextual menus, sheets, or the next step of a progressive flow.

### Calm does not mean empty

Whitespace should clarify groups and reading order. Empty space is intentional when it protects focus, not an excuse to hide essential context.

### Editorial warmth, operational precision

- Serif: welcome moments, major empty states, concise page statements, and selected evidence quotes.
- Sans: navigation, controls, metadata, tables, forms, chat, and settings.
- Mr Whale adds personality through language and small animation, not a childish full-screen theme.

### Regrade owns its identity

The app may learn from Wispr Flow's restraint while retaining Regrade's blue identity, logo, evidence colors, academic terminology, and Mr Whale. Blue becomes a precise accent rather than a full-screen gradient.

### Progressive disclosure

Upload ingredients, connector details, appeal evidence, advanced settings, and destructive actions should be revealed when relevant. The user should never parse every option before beginning.

### Trust is a visual feature

Uncertainty, consent, usage, notification behavior, automation boundaries, and supervisor access must be easy to inspect. Trust language is short, placed next to the action it qualifies, and never buried only in legal text.

## Visual direction

- Pale neutral canvas with low chroma.
- White or near-white grouped surfaces in light mode; graphite grouped surfaces in dark mode.
- Deep ink text, medium gray secondary text, Regrade blue accent, green success, amber caution, red destructive.
- Hairline dividers and minimal shadows.
- Consistent 16/20/24 px radii rather than every surface being a pill.
- Touch targets at least 44 px.
- Comfortable mobile page gutters and a bounded desktop reading width.

## Voice and copy

- Lead with a verb or direct outcome.
- Page descriptions should usually fit one line on desktop and two on mobile.
- Prefer “Review an exam” over “Review past marked exams and prepare for what is to come.”
- Prefer “Possible grading issue” over unsupported certainty.
- Keep Mr Whale conversational but not flippant when discussing grades.
